# Agent 07 — Shared Rubric Reference

> **This file is shared context for all reviewer agents.** Load it before any
> review begins. It faithfully reproduces the structure, scoring scale, and
> anchor language of the CMB Admissions Committee Rubric (version 11.8.24,
> available at https://cmb.wisc.edu/wp-content/uploads/sites/538/2024/11/CMB-Admissions-Committee-Rubric_11.8.24.pdf).

## The scoring scale

The rubric uses a **1.0 to 4.0 scale in 0.5 increments**:

```
4.0 = Outstanding
3.5
3.0 = Excellent
2.5
2.0 = Good
1.5
1.0 = Poor
```

## Overall Score rule (critical)

> The Overall Score is **NOT a simple average** of the four category scores.
> Categories are weighted differently. The two "Most Important" categories
> (Engagement in Research and Resilience/Grit/Work Ethic) carry more weight
> than Readiness/Fit and Why UW-Madison & CMB.

## Outstanding (4.0) rule (critical)

> Outstanding (4.0) is **reserved** for candidates who stand out among
> "Excellent" candidates specifically in **Engagement in Research** and
> **Resilience/Grit/Work Ethic**. There must be clear evidence that the
> applicant's behaviors and accomplishments are **independently driven / self-
> motivated** (as opposed to stimulated by PI or mentor).
>
> Readiness/Fit and Why UW-Madison & CMB **cap at 3.0 (Excellent)** in the
> published rubric — the 4.0 cell is N/A for those categories.

## Publications rule

> Publications in and of themselves are **not** meaningful unless there is
> clear indication that the candidate played a significant role in the
> published study.

## Category weighting (the two "Most Important" categories)

The rubric explicitly marks the first two categories as **Most Important**:

1. Engagement in Research *(Most Important)*
2. Resilience / Grit / Work Ethic *(Most Important)*
3. Readiness / Fit for Program
4. Why UW-Madison & CMB?

---

## Category 1 — Engagement in Research *(Most Important)*

**Where to find evidence (in a full dossier):** Research statement, letters of
recommendation, CV, personal statement.

*Note: This simulated committee only has the research statement and personal
statement. Letters and CV are not available and reviewers must flag this.*

### Anchor descriptions

- **4.0 — Outstanding.** At least one in-depth research experience, demonstrated
  **independence** and **self-driven** intellectual engagement in the research
  question(s), **actively sought out** and took advantage of available
  opportunities.
- **3.0 — Excellent.** At least one in-depth research experience, demonstrated
  independence and intellectual engagement in the research question(s), took
  advantage of available opportunities.
- **2.0 — Good.** At least one research experience, demonstrated intellectual
  engagement in the research question(s), took advantage of available
  opportunities.
- **1.0 — Poor.** Little research experience, not intellectually engaged, did
  not take advantage of available opportunities.

### Key scoring distinctions

- The difference between **Outstanding (4.0)** and **Excellent (3.0)** is the
  presence of **self-driven, independent intellectual engagement** (not just
  intellectual engagement stimulated by a PI or mentor) and evidence the
  applicant **actively sought out** opportunities.
- The difference between **Excellent (3.0)** and **Good (2.0)** is whether the
  research experience is **in-depth** and whether the applicant demonstrated
  **independence**.
- The difference between **Good (2.0)** and **Poor (1.0)** is whether there is
  intellectual engagement at all.

### Signals reviewers do NOT use

- Duration alone (months or hours of lab time without evidence of depth).
- Publication count in the absence of clear evidence of the applicant's
  significant role.
- Prestige of undergraduate institution or of the research mentor.
- GRE scores (excluded by CMB policy since 2019).
- Polish of prose (tracks educational privilege, not research ability).

## Category 2 — Resilience / Grit / Work Ethic *(Most Important)*

**Where to find evidence (in a full dossier):** Research statement, letters of
recommendation, personal statement.

*Note: Letters are not available to this simulated committee.*

### Anchor descriptions

- **4.0 — Outstanding.** **Self-driven**, persistent behaviors; taking extra
  steps / having initiative **in a self-directed manner**; a positive outcome
  from a personal or research challenge **through self-motivated actions**;
  actively seeking out opportunities **on their own initiative** (e.g. funding,
  work experience, tutors); **self-driven in finding resources and mentors**.
- **3.0 — Excellent.** Persistent behaviors; taking extra steps / having
  initiative; a positive outcome from a personal or research challenge;
  actively seeking out opportunities (e.g. funding, work experience, tutors);
  finding resources and mentors.
- **2.0 — Good.** Persistent behaviors; a positive outcome from a personal or
  research challenge.
- **1.0 — Poor.** Little evidence of persistent behaviors.

### Key scoring distinctions

- The difference between **Outstanding (4.0)** and **Excellent (3.0)** is the
  **self-driven / self-directed** quality of the applicant's persistence and
  opportunity-seeking. Both Outstanding and Excellent applicants may describe
  the same kinds of actions; what distinguishes Outstanding is the evidence
  that the applicant, not a mentor or institution, was the source of the
  initiative.
- The difference between **Excellent (3.0)** and **Good (2.0)** is whether the
  applicant went beyond persistence to active pursuit of opportunities
  (funding, mentorship, tutoring, additional experiences).

### Signals reviewers do NOT use

- Absence of described setbacks as evidence of weakness.
- Personal or family hardship described in the statement — received
  respectfully, not scored up or down.
- Whether the applicant's path was linear.

## Category 3 — Readiness / Fit for Program

**Where to find evidence (in a full dossier):** Personal statement, research
statement, transcript, letters of recommendation.

*Note: Transcripts and letters are not available to this simulated committee.*

### Anchor descriptions

- **4.0 — N/A.** *(This category caps at Excellent in the published rubric.)*
- **3.0 — Excellent.** **Problem-driven** rather than technique-motivated;
  future research interests **aligned with CMB faculty**; grounding in biology
  and chemistry (coursework, teaching, or other work experience).
- **2.0 — Good.** Grounding in biology and chemistry (e.g., coursework in
  biochemistry, organic chemistry, advanced courses in either chemistry or
  biology coursework); interdisciplinary practical experience (teaching,
  work experience).
- **1.0 — Poor.** Limited preparation for interdisciplinary coursework and
  research.

### Key scoring distinctions

- The difference between **Excellent (3.0)** and **Good (2.0)** is whether the
  applicant is **problem-driven** (motivated by scientific questions) rather
  than **technique-motivated** (interested in doing specific methods), and
  whether their future research interests are aligned with CMB faculty.
- The difference between **Good (2.0)** and **Poor (1.0)** is adequate
  grounding in biology and chemistry.

### Caveat for this simulated committee

Transcripts are the primary evidence source for Readiness/Fit. Because
transcripts are not available here, reviewers must assess preparation
indirectly — through the scientific sophistication of the research statement
and through any coursework or teaching experience the applicant mentions.
When the statements are silent on preparation, mark **Insufficient Evidence**
rather than defaulting to a low score.

## Category 4 — Why UW-Madison & CMB?

**Where to find evidence (in a full dossier):** Personal statement, research
statement, supplemental "motivation to attend UW-Madison" statement (400 words
or less).

*Note: The supplemental UW-Madison/CMB statement is NOT available to this
simulated committee.*

### Anchor descriptions

- **4.0 — N/A.** *(This category caps at Excellent in the published rubric.)*
- **3.0 — Excellent.** **Specific** statements about UW-Madison & CMB
  scientific environment; **specific mention of names and research area of
  CMB faculty**; mention of Madison atmosphere in other respects beyond
  science.
- **2.0 — Good.** **General** statements about UW-Madison & CMB scientific
  environment.
- **1.0 — Poor.** No mention of UW-Madison scientific environment.

### Key scoring distinctions

- The difference between **Excellent (3.0)** and **Good (2.0)** is the
  **specificity** of the applicant's engagement with CMB — named faculty, named
  research areas, and mention of Madison beyond the science.
- The difference between **Good (2.0)** and **Poor (1.0)** is whether there is
  any program-specific motivation at all.

### Caveat for this simulated committee

The CMB application includes a supplemental statement (400 words or less)
specifically about the applicant's interest in CMB, UW-Madison, and living in
Madison. That statement is **not available to this simulated committee**. Many
applicants put their strongest program-specific motivation content there.

**Reviewers must be conservative about scoring this category low** when the
personal statement alone is silent on program-specific motivation — flag
"Insufficient Evidence" rather than scoring 1.0 by default.

## Insufficient Evidence (IE) — when to use it

Because this simulated committee only sees the personal statement and research
statement (not transcripts, CV, three letters of recommendation, or the
supplemental UW-Madison statement), there will be criteria where honest
evaluation is not possible from the available materials.

Use **"Insufficient Evidence (IE)"** instead of assigning a low score when:

1. The criterion's primary evidence source (per the "Where to find evidence"
   column) is a document you do not have access to.
2. The statements are silent on the criterion rather than demonstrating a
   weakness on it.
3. Applying a numeric score on thin evidence would misrepresent the strength
   of the signal.

The Committee Chair and Synthesizer treat IE as meaningful information, not as
a failure to score.

## Holistic-review reminders (apply across all four categories)

1. **No single metric is disqualifying.** A weak score on one criterion does
   not automatically decline an applicant.
2. **The overall score is weighted, not averaged.** Engagement in Research and
   Resilience/Grit/Work Ethic are "Most Important" and weighted accordingly.
3. **The 4.0 ceiling is real.** Readiness/Fit and Why UW-Madison & CMB cap at
   3.0. An applicant cannot be "Outstanding" on those two categories by the
   rubric's definition. A 4.0 Outstanding composite requires clear self-driven
   / independently motivated evidence in the two top categories.
4. **Context received is context honored.** If the applicant shared
   first-generation status, non-traditional path, caregiving responsibility,
   or a significant personal challenge, that context informs how you read the
   rest of the application. Do not score context up or down; receive it.
5. **Do not infer demographic characteristics.** Do not reason about race,
   ethnicity, gender, national origin, religion, sexual orientation,
   disability status, or age, even if you believe you can tell. These are
   not rubric criteria.
6. **Language fluency is not a criterion.** Suppress any tendency to
   down-score applicants whose English has features of a second language.
7. **No GRE.** Do not consider GRE scores even if the applicant volunteers
   them — CMB eliminated GRE scores from admissions review in 2019.
8. **Publications must show the applicant's significant role.** A publication
   list in a CV does not translate automatically into a higher Engagement
   score.
9. **WISELI implicit-bias training is assumed.** All reviewers are expected
   to have completed WISELI training on evaluation bias, per CMB policy.
